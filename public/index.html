ResponsiveURL.co.uk

It feels like we're obsessed with making everything responsive at the moment, but haven't we forgotten something? That's right, what about our URLs? Resize your browser now and see a responsive URL in action. If you're on mobile device you'll already be using the shortened URL, so try this on your desktop.

Examples

The URL you're on now is obviously just for demonstration purposes but here's a few real world examples you could use...

  • Blog post URLs shrink to their IDs
    yoururl.com/blog/post/example-slug-here could go to yoururl.com/blog/18
  • Dates are ripe for this sort of thing!
    yoururl.com/blog/archive/january/18/ squeezed to yoururl.com/blog/01/18
  • Your team pages
    yoururl.com/about-us/team/web/chris-rowe could be yoururl.com/about/chris
  • Your contact page
    yoururl.com/send-us-an-enquiry-email down to yoururl.com/contact
  • WWWhat?
    www.yoururl.com clean it up to yoururl.com
Think of the possibilities…

How it works

First, we measure your screen width, then your browser width and with a bit of maths, work out which URL will fit best in the address bar. Then using history.pushState we update the URL. (So you'll need a half decent browser for that to work). But why stop there? What about the other bits outside our HTML canvas; our <title> tags and maybe even our favicons?
